📈 SOP-303: ABC Classification Standards

Standard Operating Procedure for ABC Inventory Classification

Purpose

To establish systematic ABC classification criteria that prioritize inventory management efforts based on value, velocity, and strategic importance.

Classification Criteria

Primary Factors (80% Weight)

  • Annual dollar volume (usage × unit cost)
  • Profit margin contribution
  • Annual usage frequency
  • Customer demand consistency

Secondary Factors (20% Weight)

  • Lead time variability
  • Supplier reliability
  • Storage requirements and costs
  • Obsolescence risk
  • Strategic importance to customers

Classification Definitions

Class A Items (20% of items, 80% of value)

  • High annual dollar volume (>$50,000)
  • Critical to operations or customer satisfaction
  • Require tight inventory controls
  • Monthly cycle counting
  • Formal purchase approvals required
  • Safety stock carefully calculated

Class B Items (30% of items, 15% of value)

  • Medium annual dollar volume ($10,000-$50,000)
  • Moderate importance to operations
  • Standard inventory controls
  • Quarterly cycle counting
  • Streamlined purchasing process
  • Economic order quantity optimization

Class C Items (50% of items, 5% of value)

  • Low annual dollar volume (<$10,000)
  • Lower impact on operations
  • Simplified inventory controls
  • Semi-annual cycle counting
  • Blanket purchase orders acceptable
  • Higher safety stock levels tolerable

Special Classifications

Class S (Strategic Items)

  • Critical items regardless of value
  • Single source or limited suppliers
  • Long lead times or supply constraints
  • Safety or regulatory requirements
  • Managed like Class A items

Class O (Obsolete/Slow-Moving)

  • No usage in past 12 months
  • Declining demand trend
  • Product discontinuation
  • Candidates for liquidation
  • Special handling and review

Class N (New Items)

  • Items with less than 12 months history
  • Temporary classification until data available
  • Managed conservatively until proven
  • Monthly review for reclassification

Classification Process

Data Collection

  • Extract 12 months of usage data from QR Legends system
  • Calculate annual dollar volume for each item
  • Review supplier and lead time information
  • Assess customer criticality ratings

Analysis and Scoring

  • Rank items by annual dollar volume
  • Apply weighted scoring for secondary factors
  • Calculate cumulative percentages
  • Assign preliminary classifications

Review and Adjustment

  • Management review of classifications
  • Override based on strategic importance
  • Document exceptions and rationale
  • Update classifications in system

Management Policies by Class

Ordering Policies

  • Class A: EOQ with safety stock optimization
  • Class B: EOQ with standard safety stock
  • Class C: Min/Max or blanket orders
  • Review and adjust quarterly

Storage Policies

  • Class A: Prime locations near shipping
  • Class B: Standard warehouse locations
  • Class C: Remote or high-density storage
  • Optimize based on movement frequency

Review Frequency

  • Class A: Weekly inventory review
  • Class B: Monthly inventory review
  • Class C: Quarterly inventory review
  • Annual classification update

Performance Monitoring

  • Monitor service levels by classification
  • Track inventory turns by class
  • Measure stockout frequency
  • Analyze carrying cost distribution
  • Review classification effectiveness quarterly
